Anggora Cat

Many people still have difficulty distinguishing between Anggora cats and Persian cats, even though these two types of cats are different. Soft, long, and dense fur may make Anggora difficult to distinguish from Persian cats. If Persian cats are fat, it is different from Anggora cats which tend to have a thinner body shape.

Anggora cats are characterized by a triangular head shape with dense fur resembling a lion with a nose that is more pointed than other types of cats. The neck with long fur is also a characteristic that cannot be diverted from Anggora cats. That's what makes this type of cat so adorable.

How to care for this type of cat is not much different from caring for Persian cats. Because of its dense and long fur, the owner must be diligent in paying attention to the fur and combing it. Anggora cats are usually sold at a fairly expensive price of around 1 million to 2 million.

Persian Cat

Persian cats became the first foreign-bred cat breed imported into Thailand. This cat breed is also one of the most popular cat breeds used as pets both locally and internationally. Persian cats have a broadly rounded head and forehead and small ears with rounded tips. The shape of the tail is short and the legs are short.

Persian cats have a friendly and sociable character, so they are suitable as playmates. This type of cat also has gentle, friendly, relaxed jovial, and intelligent habits. Its adorable and unique features make Persian cats the favorite of cat lovers.

The origin of the Persian Cat breed is Persia which is currently Turkey and Iran. There are ancient documents stating that Persian Cats were first introduced in Europe as Turkish Anggora Cats. They then interbred with other cat breeds resulting in the type of Cat that was registered as the Persian Cat in the UK.

How to care for this breed of cat requires special treatment because of its long and dense fur. If you keep this type of cat, you must be diligent in combing and brushing their hair every day to avoid tangles. Short-faced cats like this Persian Cat also often appear stained due to tears. So the owner must also be diligent in cleaning their eyes to prevent serious skin problems in the cat.

To buy this type of cat you need to spend more because Persian cats are usually sold at a high price of up to 13 million. But for mixed breeds, Anggora cats can be obtained at prices starting at just 500 thousand.

Sphynx Cat

Sphynx cats are one of the unique breeds of cats because they have almost no fur at all. In Australia, Sphynx cats are the only cat breed known to have no fur. But actually Sphynx Cats still have very short fur and almost resemble skin. Uniquely the Sphynx Cat is also the only cat breed that has sweat glands. It is because of this uniqueness that Sphynx Cats are much hunted by cat lovers.

This type of cat is characterized by a slender body with triangular ears, wrinkled forehead skin, and a strong chest. The tail is small and long and some cats of this breed have whiskers. Sphynx have an affectionate nature and can get along with other pets.

This pet must be bathed regularly to keep its coat healthy. This is because the Sphynx Cat's skin must be kept cool with its sweat glands. You can buy this type of cat with a price range ranging from 14 million to tens of millions.

Munchkin Cat

Munchkin cats are characterized by a unique body shape, which is a small body despite their age. The shape of the legs is short and tiny. The character of the cat's nature is quite quiet and spoiled. Munchkin cats are very suitable to be adorable pets. Original Munchkin cats can be priced up to 6 Millions, while for mixed ones the price starts from 900 thousands.

Unique Facts

1. Spends 70% of its life sleeping

Why do cats spend 70% of their lives / 15-20 hours per day just sleeping? Cats as small predatory animals living in the midst of humans need more energy to hunt. Sleeping becomes one of the ways cats store their energy.

2. Cat's Ears Can Rotate 180°

This one pet has the ability to rotate its ears up to 180°, Cats have 32 muscle tissues that can control their ears. With that much muscle, cats are able to move their ears without turning their heads to the source of the sound they hear.

3. Able to Jump 6 Times Body Length

A cat in good health can jump 6 times its body length. It often uses these leaps when hunting after birds, butterflies and dragonflies to follow its instincts.
